Common questions

Is this a rip-and-replace?

No. Works around your existing Bullhorn, Crelate, or Loxo workflows.

Do I have to migrate ATS?

No. The audit reads what your team already does and finds where revenue leaks around it.

What happens on the 20-minute call?

Walk through your placement pipeline, identify 2–3 leakage hypotheses, decide if a paid audit is worth your time.

What access do you need?

For the audit: read access to a sampling of stale-candidate threads and recent placements. NDA available. No credentials shared on the call itself.

What’s the 14-day audit output?

A leak map (where, how much, what to fix), one fix shipped, and a 30-day recovery experiment plan.

How much team time does the audit need?

Roughly 60–90 minutes total: a 30-minute kickoff to walk us through your pipeline, an async 30-minute review of the leak map mid-audit, and a 30-minute readout at the end. Outside that we work async.

What can “one fix shipped” actually look like?

Examples: an automated stale-candidate reactivation sequence; a missed-follow-up alert when a recruiter goes quiet on a placement; a client-submission status digest that runs daily; a CRM-cleanup pass that re-surfaces buried opportunities. The audit picks whichever has the highest dollar recovery for your specific pipeline.

Does this work with our current ATS?

Yes. Bullhorn, Crelate, and Loxo are the most common; Greenhouse, JobAdder, and others work too. The audit reads what your team already does inside the ATS — we don’t replace it.

What if we don’t move forward with the sprint after the audit?

You keep the leak map and the experiment plan. Some firms ship the top fix themselves with the spec we deliver. The audit isn’t a tripwire for the sprint — there’s no auto-charge and no pressure on the readout call.

What if you don’t find significant leakage?

That itself is the answer. The audit fee is the cost of finding out, not a bet on a guaranteed outcome.
